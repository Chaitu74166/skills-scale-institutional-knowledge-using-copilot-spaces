# Role Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose
Guide for onboarding new team members into OctoAcme project roles. This checklist ensures consistent, comprehensive onboarding and reduces dependency on individual team members for knowledge transfer.

---

## Pre-Onboarding (1-2 weeks before start)

- [ ] Send welcome email with project overview, key contacts, and access instructions
- [ ] Create user accounts (GitHub, project management tools, chat, etc.)
- [ ] Provision access to repositories, documentation, and shared systems
- [ ] Assign an onboarding buddy or mentor from existing team
- [ ] Schedule kickoff meeting with Project Manager and team lead
- [ ] Prepare workspace and equipment

---

## Day 1: Welcome & Setup

- [ ] Welcome meeting with Project Manager and key team members
- [ ] Review company values, psychological safety principles, and team norms
- [ ] Confirm access to all required systems and tools
- [ ] Review confidentiality, security, and compliance requirements
- [ ] Introduction to the broader organization and stakeholders
- [ ] Project overview: mission, objectives, and current status
- [ ] Review organizational structure and reporting lines

---

## Week 1: Foundational Knowledge

### Documents to Review
- [ ] OctoAcme Project Management Overview (`docs/octoacme-project-management-overview.md`)
- [ ] Current Project Charter / One-pager
- [ ] Project Roadmap and Release Plan
- [ ] Roles and Personas document (`docs/octoacme-roles-and-personas.md`)
- [ ] Team communication norms and meeting schedule

### Meetings to Attend
- [ ] Project kickoff or all-hands (if applicable)
- [ ] Team standup (observe and ask clarifying questions)
- [ ] Weekly PM/PdM sync (if relevant to role)
- [ ] Role-specific onboarding session with team lead

### System Setup
- [ ] Clone and configure necessary repositories
- [ ] Access project management tools and boards
- [ ] Subscribe to relevant communication channels
- [ ] Confirm access to documentation, wikis, and shared drives
- [ ] Set up notifications and alerts

---

## Week 2-4: Role-Specific Onboarding

### For Developers
- [ ] Review architecture and technical documentation
- [ ] Set up local development environment
- [ ] Run existing test suites and CI/CD pipeline
- [ ] Pick a small, well-scoped first task or bug fix
- [ ] Complete code review process on first PR
- [ ] Attend code review and architecture discussion sessions
- [ ] Review Definition of Done and PR submission guidelines
- [ ] Access to on-call rotations and incident playbooks (if applicable)

### For Product Managers
- [ ] Review customer and user research documents
- [ ] Attend customer discovery or research sessions (if available)
- [ ] Review current backlog, prioritization rationale, and success metrics
- [ ] Meet with Product Lead and stakeholders individually
- [ ] Understand roadmap and release timeline
- [ ] Review analytics dashboard and key success metrics
- [ ] Participate in backlog prioritization meeting

### For Project Managers
- [ ] Review project timeline, milestones, and dependencies
- [ ] Audit current risk register and mitigation plans
- [ ] Review project communications and stakeholder list
- [ ] Participate in planning and status meetings
- [ ] Understand escalation paths and decision-making authority
- [ ] Review project templates (charters, plans, retrospectives)
- [ ] Access to project dashboards and reporting tools

### For UX Designers
- [ ] Review design system, patterns, and brand guidelines
- [ ] Access design tools and shared design libraries
- [ ] Review current product user flows and prototypes
- [ ] Attend design review session
- [ ] Meet with Product Manager to understand user personas
- [ ] Review accessibility and inclusive design standards
- [ ] Understand design process and stakeholder feedback loops

### For QA Leads
- [ ] Review test strategy, test plans, and test coverage targets
- [ ] Access test management tools and CI/CD quality gates
- [ ] Review current defect backlog and triage process
- [ ] Run existing test suites (automated and manual, if applicable)
- [ ] Meet with Developers to understand testing architecture
- [ ] Review Definition of Done and acceptance criteria standards
- [ ] Understand release readiness criteria and go/no-go decision process

### For DevOps Engineers
- [ ] Review infrastructure architecture and deployment pipelines
- [ ] Access CI/CD systems, monitoring, and alerting dashboards
- [ ] Review operational runbooks and incident playbooks
- [ ] Understand environment strategy (dev, staging, production)
- [ ] Set up access to cloud platforms and infrastructure tools
- [ ] Attend on-call rotation and incident response training
- [ ] Review deployment processes and rollback procedures

### For Business Analysts
- [ ] Review requirements documentation and backlog
- [ ] Meet with Product Manager to understand business vision
- [ ] Review current user stories and acceptance criteria
- [ ] Attend requirements workshop or stakeholder session (if scheduled)
- [ ] Understand change management and scope control process
- [ ] Review traceability tools and documentation standards
- [ ] Participate in backlog refinement session

---

## Month 1-2: Integration & Ramp-Up

### General
- [ ] Attend retrospective and contribute observations
- [ ] Deliver first independent task or contribution
- [ ] Schedule 1-on-1 with Project Manager and team lead
- [ ] Provide feedback on onboarding experience
- [ ] Confirm understanding of role responsibilities and interactions
- [ ] Ask clarifying questions about gaps in knowledge

### Meetings & Milestones
- [ ] Participate actively in standups and team meetings
- [ ] Understand current project priorities and blockers
- [ ] Identify opportunities to contribute or add value
- [ ] Shadow experienced team members on complex activities

### Feedback & Adjustment
- [ ] Receive feedback from team on collaboration and performance
- [ ] Adjust expectations and workload based on ramp-up progress
- [ ] Identify additional training or mentoring needs
- [ ] Review 30-day progress and upcoming responsibilities

---

## Month 3: Full Participation

- [ ] Lead or own a significant task, story, or initiative
- [ ] Independently execute core responsibilities for your role
- [ ] Mentor or help onboard another team member
- [ ] Contribute to process improvements or documentation
- [ ] Schedule 90-day check-in with manager
- [ ] Clarify longer-term goals and career development

---

## Ongoing Support

### Regular Check-ins
- [ ] Monthly 1-on-1s with manager or team lead
- [ ] Quarterly feedback and goal reviews
- [ ] Participation in team retrospectives and continuous improvement

### Knowledge Sharing
- [ ] Update documentation based on onboarding experience
- [ ] Contribute to improving onboarding for future team members
- [ ] Share knowledge in team discussions and documentation

### Professional Development
- [ ] Identify skills gaps and training opportunities
- [ ] Participate in internal knowledge-sharing sessions
- [ ] Attend external training or conferences (as applicable)

---

## Onboarding Completion Checklist

**Manager/Team Lead Sign-off:**
- [ ] New team member has completed all core onboarding activities
- [ ] New team member demonstrates understanding of role and responsibilities
- [ ] New team member has delivered first independent contribution
- [ ] New team member has integrated with team and understands communication norms
- [ ] New team member is cleared for independent work in their domain

**Onboarding Buddy/Mentor Sign-off:**
- [ ] Mentee questions have been answered
- [ ] Mentee has navigated systems and tools successfully
- [ ] Mentee understands team dynamics and psychological safety practices

**New Team Member Confirmation:**
- [ ] I have completed onboarding and understand my role and responsibilities
- [ ] I have reviewed key documentation and project information
- [ ] I am confident in executing core aspects of my role
- [ ] I know how to ask for help and escalate issues
- [ ] I understand team communication norms and processes

---

## Notes for Onboarding Buddy/Mentor
- Be patient and encouraging; ramp-up takes time
- Check in daily during first week, then 2-3x per week for first month
- Point to documentation first, then offer clarification or context
- Celebrate milestones and contributions
- Encourage questions and provide psychological safety feedback
- Provide constructive feedback on collaboration and performance
